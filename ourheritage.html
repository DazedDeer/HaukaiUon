Our Heritage




How we incorportate Te Ao Māori

At Haukai we incorportate principles of Te Ao Māori (the Māori World), this includes using karakia to bless the food for our guests. All our staff have qualifications in Te Reo Māori so we are thrilled to be able to take bookings in Te Reo Māori as well as English.


Our Karakia

Whakapaigna ēnei kai

Hei oranga mō te tinana

Mō ō mātou wairua hoki

Āmine

Bless these foods

for the goodness of our bodies

And for our spirits as well

Amen



Traditional Recipees

Our recipes are tradional recipees that are often made as Māori dishes, we use ingredients that are local. All our fish are caught here, in the Bay of Islands. We use New Zealand lamb and plants endemic to New Zealand

Our Kawakawa crumb (used on our lamb steak) is made from the endemic to New Zealand kawakawa plant. It is a beautiful plant picked fresh from our garden that tastes similar to basil.

Our venison is crusted with horopito, which is another plant endemic to New Zealand that we pick fresh from our garden. It has a lovely peppery taste and it used to be used to treat wounds, so not only is it tastely, but it has health benefits!





Educational Material

To learn more about Te Ao Māori, pay a visit to our marae, we have plenty of signage up on the topic, as well as Educational brochures

We are close to several other sites in the bay of islands that would also be worth visiting to learn more about Te Ao Māori:

  • The Waitangi Treaty Grounds
  • The Bay of Islands Visitor Information Center
  • Urupukapuka Island
  • Haruru Falls
